# Stripe Outreach Message Bank

Last researched: 2026-06-01

## How To Use

Apply first, then send one role-specific note through a public professional channel such as LinkedIn. Keep it under 120 words. Replace bracketed fields before sending.

## Backend Engineer, AI Security

Channel: public technical recruiter profile, AI/security recruiter, or AI Security hiring-team engineer.

First note:

Hi [Name], I applied for Stripe's Backend Engineer, AI Security role. The part that pulled me in is the chance to build defenses around AI threat detection, prompt injection, and policy evaluation. My strongest fit is [1-line proof: security/infrastructure/AI system]. If this is near your hiring area, I would value guidance on the best way to make my application useful to the team.

Follow-up:

Hi [Name], quick follow-up on the AI Security role. I added a tighter 3-bullet summary of my fit: [proof 1], [proof 2], [proof 3]. If another recruiter owns this search, I would appreciate being pointed to the right public channel.

First note:

My best fit points are [project], [technical strength], and [impact]. If there is a recommended campus or public recruiting channel I should use, I would appreciate the pointer.

## Referral Ask

Use only when there is a real connection.

Hi [Name], I am applying to Stripe for [Role]. I noticed your work on [team/product/post], and it overlaps with [your relevant work]. I do not want to presume a referral, but if my background looks relevant after a quick scan, I would appreciate your guidance or a referral. Three fit points: [proof 1]; [proof 2]; [proof 3].

## What Not To Send

- Do not ask for "any job at Stripe."
- Do not mention scraped emails, guessed email formats, or contact databases.
- Do not message five recruiters for the same role at once.
- Do not use official application-support or accommodations emails for general outreach.
