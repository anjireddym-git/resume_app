# Stripe Recruiting Outreach Plan

Last researched: 2026-06-01

## Boundary

This plan intentionally avoids private recruiter email harvesting, guessed email patterns, contact-data vendors, stale personal emails from old forum posts, or anything that looks like spam. The useful route is: apply through Stripe, use public professional channels, ask for referrals where there is a real connection, and keep every message short and specific.

## What I Can Do With Available Capabilities

- Search and summarize current public Stripe jobs, public recruiter profiles, official Stripe pages, and public posts.
- Build a target tracker with role URLs, public channels, and recommended next actions.
- Draft LinkedIn notes, referral asks, follow-ups, and application-support emails.
- Tailor your resume and outreach copy once you provide your real resume or LinkedIn.
- Prepare Gmail-ready email drafts if you provide an allowed recipient and authorize sending through your own account.

## What I Need From You

- Your real resume or LinkedIn profile.
- Target role family: backend, infrastructure, security, AI/ML, data, product, sales, university/new grad, etc.
- Preferred locations: Remote US, Chicago, Seattle, New York, South San Francisco, India, Dublin, etc.
- Seniority: intern, new grad, mid-level, senior, staff, manager.
- 3 to 5 Stripe role URLs you like, or permission for me to shortlist from Stripe Jobs.
- Any Stripe employees, alumni, school contacts, or investor/founder network ties you already have.

## Outreach Sequence

1. Apply directly through the exact Stripe role page.
2. Tailor resume bullets to the role's language and team needs.
3. Send one LinkedIn note to the most relevant public recruiter or a hiring-team employee.
4. Ask for a referral only when there is a real shared context.
5. Follow up once after 5 to 7 business days with new signal, not pressure.

## Role Routing

- Backend/API, infrastructure, security, AI/ML engineering: AMER/APAC technical recruiting based on location, plus hiring-team engineers.
- Data analyst, data science, ML underwriting: data recruiting or data/ML hiring-team employees.
- Product design, UX research, design engineering: design recruiting.
- Internships and new grad roles: university recruiting.
- Operations / Stripe Delivery Center roles: operations recruiting.
- Product manager roles: PM hiring-team employees first, then recruiters who publicly post about PM hiring.

## Message Templates

### Recruiter LinkedIn Connection

Hi {Name}, I applied for {Role} at Stripe because the team is working on {specific team/problem}. My background is in {2-3 word positioning}, especially {specific proof}. If this sits near your hiring area, I would value any guidance on the best way to be considered.

### After Applying

Hi {Name}, quick follow-up: I applied for {Role} ({job ID if visible}). The part that stood out is {specific line from JD}. I have done related work in {specific project/system/result}. If useful, I can send a tighter 3-bullet summary for the hiring team.

### Referral Ask

Hi {Name}, I am applying to Stripe for {Role}. I noticed your work on {team/product/post}. I do not want to presume a referral, but if my background looks relevant after a quick scan, I would appreciate your guidance or a referral. Here are the three strongest fit points: {bullet 1}; {bullet 2}; {bullet 3}.

### Application Technical Issue

Subject: Trouble submitting Stripe application for {Role}

Hi Stripe candidate support team,

I am trying to submit my application for {Role} at {job URL}, but I am hitting {specific issue}. I tried {browser/device/steps}. Could you help me complete the application?

Thank you,
{Your name}

## Rules Of Engagement

- Keep messages under 120 words.
- Mention one role, not "any role."
- Never say you used scraping, guessed emails, or contact databases.
- Do not mass-message multiple recruiters with the same note.
- If a recruiter replies, stop prospecting other recruiters for that same role unless they redirect you.
